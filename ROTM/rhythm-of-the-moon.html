A gentle story about growing up

In The Rhythm of the Moon, we meet Elara — a girl who feels the world very deeply. Sometimes sounds feel too loud, emotions feel overwhelming, and changes can feel scary.

One day Elara experiences something new — her first period. With the loving guidance of her mother Sylvia, she begins to understand that her body is changing in a natural way. Through patience, reassurance, and simple calming tools, Elara learns that her body has its own rhythm — just like the moon in the sky.

Inside the book

Inside the story, children follow Elara as she learns about body changes, big emotions, and the calming rhythm of her own cycle. These pages introduce the moon cycle metaphor, comfort tools, and the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding method.

What children will learn

  • That body changes are natural and nothing to be ashamed of
  • How the menstrual cycle can be explained in a child-friendly way
  • That big feelings can happen during change and growth
  • Simple grounding tools to help the body feel calm again

Perfect for

About the author

Amanda Carleton is the creator of NeuroVibe and NeuroVibe Publishing. She creates supportive resources, stories, and tools for families who want more understanding, encouragement, and calm through everyday challenges.

I wrote this book for my daughters, Liana and Althea. My hope is that this story helps children understand their bodies, their feelings, and the journey of growing up a little more with confidence and kindness.

A story with heart

The Rhythm of the Moon combines gentle parenting, neurodivergent support, body literacy, and emotional regulation into a warm, approachable story children can understand.

It reminds young readers that their feelings are real, their bodies are amazing, and they are never alone while growing through change.

Help your child grow with confidence

Help your child understand growing up with confidence, calm, and support. The Rhythm of the Moon gives families a gentle way to talk about body changes, emotions, and the natural rhythm of growing up.
