The Rhythm of the Moon

A gentle story about growing up, big feelings, and understanding your body. Follow Elara as she learns that the changes she is experiencing are part of a natural rhythm — just like the phases of the moon.

About the Story

In The Rhythm of the Moon, we meet Elara — a girl who feels the world very deeply. Sometimes sounds feel too loud, emotions feel overwhelming, and change can feel scary.

One day Elara experiences something new — her first period. With the loving guidance of her mother Sylvia, she begins to understand that her body is changing in a natural way. Through patience, reassurance, and simple calming tools, Elara learns that her body has its own rhythm — just like the moon in the sky.

Inside the Book

Inside the story, children follow Elara as she learns about body changes, big emotions, and the calming rhythm of her own cycle.

Elara learns that her body changes just like the phases of the moon.

Sylvia gently explains how our bodies have their own rhythm.

Elara practices the 5-4-3-2-1 calm method to settle big feelings.

What Children Will Learn

  • That body changes are natural and nothing to fear
  • How the menstrual cycle can be explained in a child-friendly way
  • How emotions can feel bigger during times of change
  • Simple grounding tools to calm the nervous system

About the Author

Amanda Carleton is the creator of NeuroVibe and NeuroVibe Publishing. She creates supportive resources, stories, and tools for families who want more understanding and encouragement in everyday life.

This book was written for her daughters, Liana and Althea, with the hope that it helps children understand their bodies, their feelings, and the journey of growing up with confidence.
